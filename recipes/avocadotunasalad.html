Avocado Tuna Salad

Here is a recipe for an avocado tuna salad that should help you stay in ketosis:

Recipe description

This avocado tuna salad has a bright and refreshing flavor, thanks to the combination of lemon juice, olive oil, and fresh herbs like cilantro and jalapeno.
The avocado adds a creamy and slightly nutty note, while the vegetables add a crunchy texture and a burst of freshness. Overall, this salad is satisfying and flavorful, making it a great option for a light lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans tuna, drained
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 2 tablespoons diced bell pepper
  • 2 tablespoons diced cucumber
  • 2 tablespoons diced tomato
  • 2 tablespoons diced cilantro
  • 2 tablespoons diced jalapeno (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the tuna, avocado, red onion, bell pepper, cucumber, tomato, cilantro, and jalapeno (if using).
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice and olive oil. Pour the dressing over the tuna mixture and toss to coat.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve the salad as is, or over a bed of lettuce or spinach.

Remarks

This recipe should yield about 4 servings. Each serving has approximately 200 calories, 13 grams of fat, 9 grams of carbohydrates, and 15 grams of protein.
The net carbs per serving are about 5 grams, which should help keep you in ketosis as long as you are maintaining your daily carbohydrate intake at or below your personal carb limit.
