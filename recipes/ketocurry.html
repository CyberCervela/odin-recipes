Keto Curry

Here is a recipe for a low-carb curry that should help you stay in ketosis:

Recipe description

This low-carb curry recipe has a rich and aromatic flavor, thanks to the combination of aromatic spices like curry powder, turmeric, cumin, and coriander. The coconut milk adds a creamy and slightly sweet note, while the vegetables add a bit of crunch and freshness. Overall, the flavors in this curry are well-balanced, with a little bit of heat from the cayenne pepper (if you choose to use it) and a good amount of depth and complexity from the various spices.
In terms of texture, the chicken in this recipe is tender and moist, while the broccoli and cauliflower are both cooked to a perfect al dente consistency. The coconut milk and vegetables create a thick and creamy sauce that coats the chicken and vegetables beautifully.
Overall, this curry has a satisfying and comforting texture that is sure to please.

Ingredients:

    • 1 medium onion, diced
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
    • 1 pound chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
    • 1 tablespoon curry powder
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric
    • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
    • 1/2 teaspoon coriander
    • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
    • 1 cup coconut milk
    • 1 cup broccoli florets
    • 1 cup cauliflower florets
    • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the coconut oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger and cook until the onion is translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the chicken and cook until it is no longer pink, about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in the curry powder, turmeric, cumin, coriander, and cayenne pepper (if using). Cook for an additional minute.
  4. Add the coconut milk, broccoli, and cauliflower. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 15 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve over cauliflower rice or with a side of your choice.

Remarks

This recipe should yield about 4 servings. Each serving has approximately 270 calories, 18 grams of fat, 14 grams of carbohydrates, and 24 grams of protein. The net carbs per serving are about 5 grams, which should help keep you in ketosis as long as you are maintaining your daily carbohydrate intake at or below your personal carb limit.
